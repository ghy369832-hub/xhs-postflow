# Xiaohongshu Copy Gates

Use these gates after the evidence pack exists. The goal is a publishable draft, not a guaranteed viral post.

## Packaging Gate

Titles should usually include 2-3 of these, but avoid stuffing:
- target reader or situation;
- concrete benefit or avoided loss;
- curiosity gap;
- number or checklist;
- searchable keywords.

Avoid:
- exaggerated certainty;
- medical cure promises;
- copying the source title too closely;
- AI-ish slogans like “一文讲清楚” when the body is not deep enough.

## Body Structure

Default structure:
1. One-line pain point or scenario.
2. Why this matters, in plain language.
3. 3-5 concrete points, each with explanation and actionable wording.
4. A small caution or boundary.
5. Soft CTA: ask for the reader's situation, checklist request, or save prompt.

## Humanization Gate

Revise once to make the draft feel like a real Xiaohongshu note:
- shorter paragraphs;
- concrete daily-life scenes;
- fewer formal transitions;
- no generic AI cadence;
- no inflated claims;
- keep a conversational but credible voice.

## Originality Gate

Before final output, add a short note:
- Evidence reused from source structure:
- New adaptation introduced:
- Claims needing user/domain confirmation:

If the draft is too close to the source, rewrite with a different lead, different examples, and different ordering.
